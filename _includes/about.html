ABOUT ME

I am an Assistant Professor in Legal Technologies at Leiden University, working at the intersection of AI, law, and society. With a technical background and eight years of experience working within the legal domain, I focus on the development, deployment, and critical evaluation of algorithmic systems used in legal and other high-stakes contexts. I take a systems-level perspective on technology, focusing on how AI tools shape - and are shaped by - the institutions and contexts in which they are used. I specialise in machine learning applications and examine their potential but also the risks and constraints embedded in their design and use. More broadly, I work on responsible AI, risk assessment, and the ethics of automation. I collaborate with courts, researchers, and policy institutions to help ensure that the systems we build are not only technically sound but also aligned with broader societal values.
